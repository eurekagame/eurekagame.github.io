Eureka Quick Reference

Based on the work of the creators of Lancer's Quick Reference Rules, here's all the most important information contained in the sprawling 700-page Eureka rulebook, as of summer 2025. Clinking on any of the in-text hyperlinks will send you to our complete rule compendium with examples and play advice straight from the rulebook.

Investigative Rolls

Investigative rolls are any rolls that aim to directly uncover some piece of information that puts the investigators closer to solving the mystery. For example, bypassing a keypad to a police evidence room is a Non-Investigative Roll, finding a case file in the filing cabinet inside is an Investigative Roll.

Any skills can be used to investigate the mystery, but the GM ultimately determines which skills are plausibly applicable in which circumstances. Failure gives you investigation points, so there's no reason to only roll when you have an applicable skill bonus or argue extensively about what constitutes a plausible skill.

Investigative rolls always grant some investigation points. On such a roll points are granted as follows:

Full Success: The investigator gains 1 Investigation Point, and learns all the information they can from their roll.

Partial Success: The investigator gains 2 Investigation Points, and learns something from the roll. They may only get part of a clue, or, rarely, an unexpected consequence may happen.

Eureka! Points

A Eureka! point is granted to a character who has achieved a sufficient number of Investigation Points (15 in the default character playbook, with any extra rolling over). Eureka! points may be used for particular traits, but all characters can use them to do the following:

Eureka! Moment: Eureka! points can be spent to gain a piece of information that was lost on a previously failed Investigative Roll (or a previous Partial Success that did not reveal all the information) of the player's choice so long as it was one made by their investigator. This also restores 1 point of Composure.

Focus: Eureka! points may also be used to add an additional d6 per Eureka! spent to a Non-Investigative Roll - then drop all dice but the highest two. Use of a Eureka! point in this way must be declared before the dice are rolled. This roll still cannot be used to attempt a completely impossible outcome.

Eureka! Moments require keeping careful notes on unresolved Investigative rolls so you can come back to them later - but at the same time, if your investigator has learned the information related to the original roll another way, your GM may reveal an unplanned secret on the fly!
