Eureka Quick Reference

Based on the work of the creators of Lancer's Quick Reference Rules, here's all the most important information contained in the sprawling 700-page Eureka rulebook, as of summer 2025. Clinking on any of the in-text hyperlinks will send you to our complete rule compendium with examples and play advice straight from the rulebook.

Inventory

An investigator's inventory represents the items in their possession. How much an investigator can carry is meant to be played by ear, as there are no specific encumbrance limits or item weights in Eureka. Carrying what would clearly be an exceptionally heavy load, however, may require an Athletics roll. There are three types of inventory:

On-Person: Items carried on the investigator's person, such as in pockets, a backpack, in their hands, etc

Secured:Items owned but left in a location normally only accessible to the investigator. Possessions that are these other locations also count as secured inventory.

  • Home Secured: An investigators home and the items therein.
  • Vehicle Secured: An investigators vehicle and the items therein.

Unsecured: Items that investigators know the location of but that are not reliably in their possession in any real way.

Just because an item is in the On-Person or Secured inventory does not mean it is literally perfectly “secure.” A character can be pickpocketed, or something might be stolen out of their vehicle in a rough neighborhood, but barring circumstances like these, the character should be able to reasonably access those items.

Wealth Points

As the last step of character creation, players will roll for their investigators' Wealth Points (WP). Rather than tracking item purchases dollar by dollar, Wealth Points are an abstract way of measuring an investigator's finances at the start of the adventure. Unlike other aspects of a character sheet (which may or may not change), an investigator's starting inventory items are always wiped clean at the start of a new adventure. An investigator's starting items do not represent the sum of all their worldly possessions, just the ones they unquestionably have access to at the start of the adventure.

To determine an investigator's Wealth Points, roll 3D6+6+(Wealthx2). The result is their total budget of Wealth Points. Players can add items from the lists below to their investigators' inventories, spending the listed number of WP. Wealth Points are not a resource used or tracked during gameplay, they are only used during character creation. (Although the listed WP cost of an item does influence a Wealth roll made to purchase an item during play.)

Items of little consequence such as wallets, basic clothing, handkerchiefs, and so on are considered to have 0WP cost when determining inventory. During play, if it makes sense for a character to have something like this, but it's not directly listed on their sheet, it's usually fine to just say they have it anyway. Use your best judgement: an investigator having alcohol wipes in their purse to clean up after a picnic shouldn't call for a Wealth roll, but suddenly having alcohol pads to clean up after leaving an incriminating blood spatter at a crime scene might. Finally, just because any investigator can be armed, doesn't mean they should be. Consider whether the investigator would actually be the type to own or carry a weapon.

Item Lists

This is by no means an exhaustive list of all items that can be obtained in Eureka, but it can be used as a reference to come up with an appropriate Wealth Point cost for any item that isn't listed. Don't forget to give them a means of transportation, a food budget, and especially a home.

Secured Inventories (whether they be vehicles, or especially homes) generally come furnished with a great deal of extra stuff that the investigator might logically own or have access to. It can be appropriate to say that an investigator might have some useful item lying around. Vehicles and homes have what's called a Property Modifier (PM), which broadly represents the plausible range of items that might be found or stored within. The Property Modifier will be listed next to each home and vehicle. When making a Wealth roll to check if the investigator already has an item in a particular Secured Inventory, also apply the Property Modifier to the roll. This makes it so that a potentially useful item is more likely to be squirreled away in a closet of a middle class home than attached to a bicycle.

Animals

These costs include food and basic care requirements. We leave the details of animal ownership (as well as the exact lines between a "small," "medium," and "large" animal) up to you.

  • Small Animal (e.g. hamster): 1WP.
  • Medium Animal (e.g. dog): 2WP.
  • Large Animal (e.g. horse): 4WP.
  • Trained Animal: +1WP to the cost.
  • “Exotic” Animal: +2WP to the cost.
  • Exotic Pet License: 2WP.

Armor

Most armor will protect against melee weapons. Only bulletproof armor and military helmets will protect against firearms.

  • Biker Suit: 2WP.
  • Bulletproof Vest (Kevlar): 3WP; Can be worn under clothing, but may not protect against high calibre ammunition.
  • Hard Hat: 1WP.
  • Bulletproof Vest (Ceramic Plates): 3WP.
  • Jacket (Denim): 1 WP.
  • Jacket (Leather): 1 WP.
  • Military Helmet: 3WP.
  • Motorcycle Helmet: 1WP.
  • Stabproof Vest: 3WP.
  • Thick Winter Coat: 1WP.

Firearms

All firearms come with one empty magazine (internal or external). To load it, ammunition must also be purchased.

  • Bipod: 1WP.
  • Bullets (Box of 100): 1WP, calibre decided upon purchase. Add 1 WP to the cost for special ammunition such as hollow-point.
  • Bullet (Silver): 2WP; Calibre decided upon purchase. Only one per purchase.
  • Bump Stock: 1WP.
  • Firearms License: 2WP; Makes not-fully-automatic firearms legal to own.
  • Flashlight (Mounted): 1WP.
  • Holster: 1WP; Required for comfortably carrying a handgun on one's person.
  • Laser Sight: 1WP.
  • Machine Gun: 6WP, illegal to own in NY.
  • Machine Pistol: 4WP.
  • Magazine (External): 1WP.
  • Pistol (Semi-Automatic): 3WP.
  • Revolver (Double-Action): 3WP.
  • Revolver (Single-Action): 3WP; The hammer must be pulled back for every shot.
  • Rifle (Automatic): 8WP.
  • Rifle (Repeating): 3WP. Any rifle that must be cycled for every shot, such as a bolt-action or lever-action.
  • Rifle (Semi-Automatic): 4WP.
  • Scope: 1WP.
  • Shotgun (Automatic): 8WP.
  • Shotgun (Break-Action): 3WP; Any shotgun that must be cycled for every shot, such as a pump-action or lever-action.
  • Shotgun (Repeating): 8WP.
  • Shotgun (Sawn-Off): 3WP, illegal.
  • Shotgun (Semi-Automatic): 5WP.
  • Shotgun Shells (Box of 100): 1WP; Gauge chosen on purchase. Add 1 WP to the cost for special ammunition such as slugs.
  • Speed-Loader: 1WP; For loading revolvers quickly.
  • Stock: 1WP; Most long guns already come with this included.
  • Strap: 1WP; Long guns only. Makes it easier to carry, and harder to be disarmed.
  • Stripper Clip: 1WP; For quickly loading certain guns that have internal magazines.

Homes

“PM” listed after each entry is the Property Modifier. For hotels or other temporary lodging, each night must be purchased with WP separately in advance, then rolled for each night afterwards. For instance, an investigator could buy three nights at a cheap hotel for 3 WP, but then staying a fourth night would require a Wealth roll.

  • Apartment (Large) (PM 0): 5WP.
  • Apartment (Small) (PM -1): 3WP.
  • Apartment (Tiny) (PM -2): 2WP.
  • Camper, RV, or Trailer Home (PM -3): 2WP. If it's motorized, 3WP instead. -2 to Driving. +16 Acceleration.
  • Hotel Room (Cheap) (PM -5): 1WP per night.
  • Hotel Room (Moderate) (PM -4): 2WP per night. Complimentary Breakfast.
  • Hotel Room (Fancy) (PM -2): 3WP per night. Complimentary Breakfast.
  • House (Large) (PM +2): 10WP.
  • House (Small) (PM +1): 6WP.
  • Mansion (Large) (PM +10): 30WP.
  • Mansion (Small (for a mansion)) (PM +5): 15WP.
  • Tent (PM -7): 1WP.

Vehicles

“PM” listed after each entry is the Property Modifier. The Acceleration stat is used when determining Speed in a Chase. If an investigator is living out of their vehicle (e.g. they don't have another place to stay), apply -3 to the PM of their vehicle - they have to cram sleeping arrangements and clothes inside of a car.

  • Bicycle (Regular) (PM -6): 1WP. +1 Contextual to Athletics unless off-road. +2 Acceleration.
  • Bicycle (Off-road) (PM -6): 2WP. +1 Contextual to Athletics. No off-road penalty. +2 Acceleration.
  • Boating License: 1WP. Makes it legal to pilot boats.
  • Car (Junker) (PM -2): 2WP. -1 Driving. +16 Acceleration.
  • Car (Regular) (PM -1): 4WP. +16 to Acceleration.
  • Car (Sports) (PM -2): 8WP. +2 Contextual to Driving unless off-road. +24 Acceleration.
  • Dirtbike (PM -6): 2WP. +2 Contextual to Driving. No off-road penalty. +12 Acceleration.
  • Driver's License: 1WP. Makes it legal to drive a motor vehicle.
  • Four-Wheeler (PM -2): 2WP. No off-road penalty. +12 Acceleration.
  • Motorcycle (PM -3): 3WP. +2 Contextual to Driving unless off-road. +20 to Acceleration.
  • Motor Scooter (PM -3): 2WP. +3 Acceleration.
  • Pickup Truck (PM 0): 4WP. No off-road penalty. +16 Acceleration.
  • Public Transportation Pass: 1WP. Makes public transportation legal to ride.
  • Skateboard: 1WP. +1 Contextual to Athletics unless off-road. No Property modifier. +1 Acceleration.
  • SUV (PM 0): 5WP. No off-road penalty. +16 Acceleration.
  • Van (PM +1): 4WP. -2 to Driving. +16 Acceleration.
