Eureka Quick Reference

Based on the work of the creators of Lancer's Quick Reference Rules, here's all the most important information contained in the sprawling 700-page Eureka rulebook, as of summer 2025. Clinking on any of the in-text hyperlinks will send you to our complete rule compendium with examples and play advice straight from the rulebook.

Setting for E's Eureka Adventure

This Eureka adventure will take place in late-70s NYC (speficially the summer of 1977). Some of the players are extremely knowledgeable about dimensions of politics, music, sports, geography, while others may be less so. This is not a problem. For one thing, different people have different levels of awareness of all kinds of current events and pop culture. If you feel unprepared to play an investigator with a different level of knowledge than you (more OR less), don't!

That said, there are some very basic phenomena that your investigators would be aware of. For the player in a rush, the two minimum required texts are New York Style Magazine's 2015 article "Why Can't We Stop Talking About New York in the Late 1970s? and this Wikipedia article section titled Fiscal crisis of 1975. To avoid spoilers, read absolutely no further.

For those interested, I have compiled a list of other interesting pieces of historical context that will give you a better sense of the time or awareness of specific cultural phenomena. This list is an unhappy marriage between my instinct to recommend full book chapters, the available material easily found in today's google search engine, and the overwhelming racism and classism that characterizes both retrospective and then-current material. Inclusion is not uncritical endorsement.

Political Movements

The strength of radical identity movements waned in the late 1970s, largely as a result of infighting spurred by government espionage. These articles are curated to illustrate both the decline and the recent past that any long-term residents would be aware of.

  • Wikipedia Section - only the section under 1977-1982 is directly relevant.
  • Podcast Transcript on the significance of a Black-run Brooklyn community center and school that reflects the rise of pan-africanism and Black power in the 70s.
  • Book Review on the history of the Young Lords, a socialist Puerto Rican nationalist organization from the Bronx.
  • Study Guide from Swarthmore on the occupation of Engine Company 212 (People's Firehouse) in Brooklyn. Read "case study details".
